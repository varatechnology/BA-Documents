### Executive Summary: Step 1 – QR Code Based Initial Verification

The **Step 1: QR Code Based Initial Verification** process in Certcheck delivers instant, privacy-preserving confirmation of certificate authenticity—without exposing personal details.

When a verifier scans the QR code on a certificate, Certcheck immediately checks its validity, cryptographic integrity, and registration status on the blockchain and with the issuing institution. Only key contextual information—such as a masked candidate name, issuer, partial certificate ID, and issuance timestamp—is displayed to the verifier. All sensitive or detailed data remains hidden by default, ensuring privacy and regulatory compliance.

To proceed further, candidate consent is explicitly required. The verifier is prompted to request access, and the system cannot move to full data verification without the candidate’s approval. This design not only stops unauthorized data exposure, but also puts the certificate holder in direct control of what is shared and when.

**Key Business Outcomes:**  
- **Instant Trust:** Verifiers immediately know the certificate is real and registered, reducing turnaround time for initial checks.
- **Privacy by Design:** No personal or sensitive information can be revealed without candidate’s verified consent.
- **Clear Audit Trail:** All lookup attempts and consent requests are logged for transparency and accountability.
- **User Empowerment:** Certificate holders choose when and with whom to share their details, strengthening trust and adoption.

This step creates a strong, trust-based foundation for fast, compliant certificate verification—enabling organizations to verify without risk, and individuals to remain firmly in control of their credentials from the very first scan.
