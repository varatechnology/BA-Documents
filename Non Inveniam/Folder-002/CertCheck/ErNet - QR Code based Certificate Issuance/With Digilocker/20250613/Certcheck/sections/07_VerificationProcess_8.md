### Executive Summary: Step 2 – Full Data Verification

The improved **Step 2: Full Data Verification** process is where Certcheck’s trust infrastructure reaches its fullest potential, balancing instant, cross-system authenticity with strict privacy and user control.

Upon the verifier’s request, the candidate (student or certificate holder) is prompted through DigiLocker to provide explicit, one-time consent—secured by eAadhaar authentication. This digital approval is required before sharing any personal or sensitive credential information.

Once consent is granted, Certcheck performs an exhaustive integrity check by comparing secure, tamper-proof hashes of the document from four independent sources:
1. The issuing institution’s official records,
2. The candidate’s copy in DigiLocker,
3. The original hash embedded in the certificate’s QR code,
4. The transaction hash anchored on the blockchain.

Only if **all four sources** match exactly does Certcheck release the full credential and details to the verifier, displaying a clear “Verified: Authentic & Untampered” result. Any mismatch automatically blocks access, flags potential tampering, and triggers proactive alerts for investigation—without leaking any sensitive details.

**Key Business Outcomes:**  
- **Maximum Trust:** Multi-source verification exceeds compliance and industry standards for document integrity and authenticity.
- **Zero Data Leakage:** Candidates’ credential data remains fully protected; nothing is released without their explicit digital consent.
- **Transparent & Auditable:** Every access, action, and result is logged—empowering institutions, candidates, and verifiers with evidence and recourse.
- **Frictionless Experience:** Candidates retain control and visibility, while verifiers receive fast, actionable confirmation—including reasons for failure or next steps if results do not match.

Certcheck’s Full Data Verification step ensures that only genuinely owned, genuinely untampered credentials are ever shared or recognized—raising the bar for secure, user-centric verification on a national and global scale.
